Groundwork Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-06-30

What Groundwork collects

  • Precise location during active runs to record routes and detect closed loops.
  • Motion and fitness data such as steps and cadence during runs.
  • Profile information you choose to provide, such as display name, username, avatar, and territory color.
  • Run information such as distance, duration, pace, elevation, and claimed territory shapes.
  • Optional Apple Health data when you connect Health, including permission to write workouts and read body mass for calorie estimates.

How data is used

  • To track runs and calculate claims.
  • To show territory ownership and leaderboard positions.
  • To notify you when another player takes your land.
  • To save completed runs to Apple Health when you grant permission.

Where data is stored

Groundwork uses Apple CloudKit tied to your Apple ID.

  • Public data such as display name, username, avatar, color, territory shapes, and leaderboard standing may be visible to other Groundwork users.
  • Private run history is stored in your private CloudKit database.

Groundwork does not run its own application server for run storage and does not use ads or third-party analytics in V1.

Your controls

  • You can stop a run at any time.
  • You can disconnect Apple Health permissions in the Health app.
  • You can delete your account in-app from the profile screen. This permanently removes your profile, username, territories, and stored run history from Groundwork’s CloudKit data stores.
